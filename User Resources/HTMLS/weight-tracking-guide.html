Weight Tracking

How to weigh yourself daily without it messing with your head.

Why Weigh Daily

Your weight fluctuates constantly. If you weigh once a week, you might catch a high day and think you've gained fat. Or a low day and think you've lost more than you have. Neither is true.

Daily data gives you the full picture. One number means nothing. The trend means everything.

The Routine

1
Wake up Same time each day, give or take.
2
Use the bathroom Empty bladder at minimum. This matters more than you'd think.
3
Step on the scale Same scale, same spot on the floor, minimal clothing. Consistency is the point.
4
Log it in your Weight tab Open your meal plan, go to the Weight tab, enter the number. Takes 10 seconds.
5
Move on with your day Don't analyse it. Don't react to it. Just log it and go.

The Weekly Average

This is the concept that changes everything.

Your daily weight is noise. Your weekly average is signal. Compare this week's average to last week's average. That's the only number that matters.

Example: You weigh in at 75.2, 75.8, 75.4, 75.0, 75.6, 75.3, 75.1 across a week. Some days up, some down. Doesn't matter. Your average is 75.3kg. If last week's average was 75.8kg, you're down 0.5kg. That's real progress—even though individual days looked "bad."

Why Weight Fluctuates

Your body isn't broken. These are normal reasons your weight changes day to day:

  • Water retention — sodium, carbs, hormones, even the weather
  • Food volume — a big meal still digesting weighs something
  • Sleep — poor sleep increases water retention
  • Stress — cortisol holds onto water
  • Exercise — inflammation from training adds temporary water weight
  • Menstrual cycle — can swing weight 1-3kg through the month

None of this is fat. It's just... stuff. The trend over weeks shows what's actually happening.

The Scale Is Data

The number on the scale is not a grade. It's not a judgment of your effort, your worth, or whether you're "good" or "bad." It's information. That's it.

If the number affects your mood, that's worth paying attention to. A measurement shouldn't have that power over you. If it does, let's talk about it—because the goal is a healthy relationship with data, not dread every morning.

The clients who get the best results treat weighing like brushing their teeth. Boring. Automatic. Emotionally neutral. That's the goal.

How To Think About It

Wrong

"I'm up 0.4kg from yesterday. The diet isn't working. I'll eat less today."

Right

"I'm up 0.4kg from yesterday. Noted. My weekly average is still trending down. Moving on."

Making It Stick

Anchor it to something. Weigh yourself right after you pee, before you shower. Same sequence every day. It becomes automatic.

Keep the scale visible. Next to the toilet, in front of the shower—wherever you can't miss it. Out of sight = forgotten.

Log immediately. Don't "remember it for later." Open the Weight tab, type the number, done. 10 seconds.

Daily weight is noise. Weekly average is signal. Trust the trend.
