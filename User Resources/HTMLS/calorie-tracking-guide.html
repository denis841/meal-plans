Calorie Tracking

How to make logging take 30 seconds a day instead of 10 minutes.

Why Track At All

You can't manage what you don't measure. People who think they're eating 1,800 calories are often eating 2,400. People who think they're eating "healthy" are often eating 300 calories of olive oil without realising it.

Tracking removes the guesswork. You either hit your numbers or you didn't.

Get Set Up (Once)

1
Download MyFitnessPal Free version. iOS or Android. Create an account.
2
Set your calorie goal Go to Goals → Calorie & Macros. Enter the calories from your meal plan. Ignore MFP's suggestions.
3
Get a food scale £10-15 on Amazon. Non-negotiable. This is how you actually know what you're eating.

The Workflow That Changes Everything

Most people quit tracking because it takes forever. They search for every ingredient, every time, for every meal. That's insane.

The fix: Create your meals from your meal plan ONCE in MFP. Then logging is just selecting "Day 1 Breakfast" and adjusting the grams if needed. Done in seconds.

Creating A Meal In MFP

1
Go to "My Meals" Bottom menu → More → My Meals → Create Meal
2
Name it clearly Use "Day 1 Breakfast" or "Chicken Rice Bowl" — something you'll recognise instantly.
3
Add each ingredient from your meal plan Search for the ingredient, enter the grams listed in your plan. Repeat for all ingredients.
4
Save it Now it exists forever. To log it, just search the meal name and tap to add.

Do this for every meal in your plan. Yes, it takes 20-30 minutes upfront. But you'll spend less than 2 minutes a day logging after that. For weeks. Maybe months.

Daily Logging

Once your meals are saved, your daily routine is:

1
Weigh your food Put plate on scale, zero it, add food, note the grams.
2
Log the meal Search your saved meal name, tap to add. Adjust serving size if your portion differs.
3
Move on That's it. 30 seconds per meal.

Pro Moves

Pre-log your day. Log everything in the morning or night before. Now you've committed. You just have to eat what you already logged.

Use the barcode scanner. For packaged foods, tap the barcode icon in MFP and scan. Instant entry. No searching.

When eating out, estimate and move on. Search for something similar in MFP, pick a reasonable option, log it. Don't stress. One imperfect log doesn't ruin anything. Not logging at all does.

Why The Scale Matters

"One tablespoon of peanut butter" can be 90 calories or 180 calories depending on how generous you are. "A chicken breast" can be 120g or 250g. You don't know until you weigh it.

The scale removes the lying-to-yourself part. It takes 3 seconds and makes your data actually mean something.

Mistakes That Derail People

× Not logging cooking oils. That "healthy" stir fry has 200+ invisible calories from oil.
× Logging after the fact. You forget things. Pre-log or log as you eat.
× Eyeballing portions. Your "100g of rice" is probably 160g. Weigh it.
× Skipping "small" things. The handful of nuts, the splash of milk, the bite of someone's dessert. It adds up.
× Giving up after a bad day. One day off doesn't matter. A week off does. Just log the next meal.

20 minutes of setup buys you months of easy tracking.
