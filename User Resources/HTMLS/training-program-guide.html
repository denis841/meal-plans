How to Follow Your Program

Everything you need to know to walk into the gym and execute.

Where Your Program Lives

Your training program is in the Everfit app. Open it, tap on today's workout, and you'll see everything laid out: exercises, sets, reps, and weights. Each exercise has a demo video if you're unsure about form—tap on it and watch.

Sets and Reps

When you see something like 3x10, that means:

Example
3 x 10 = 3 sets of 10 reps

A rep is one complete movement. A set is a group of reps done together before resting. So 3x10 means: do 10 reps, rest, do 10 reps, rest, do 10 reps. Done.

Week 1: Finding Your Numbers

The first week uses RPE — Rate of Perceived Exertion. It's just a 1-10 scale of how hard a set feels:

RPE 7 = You could do 3 more reps if you had to
RPE 8 = You could do 2 more reps
RPE 9 = You could do 1 more rep
RPE 10 = Nothing left in the tank

In Week 1, you'll pick weights that hit the RPE listed for each exercise. This tells me what you can actually lift, so I can program your numbers going forward. Be honest with yourself—it doesn't help either of us if you go too light or blow yourself up.

Week 2 Onwards: Just Follow the Numbers

After Week 1, your program switches to percentage-based. The app will show you exactly what weight to lift. You don't need to calculate anything—just load the bar with what it says and do the reps.

The thinking is done for you. Your job is to show up, lift the prescribed weight for the prescribed reps, log it, and leave. Simple.

Rest Between Sets

Rest isn't always prescribed, so here's the general rule:

Compound Lifts Squat, deadlift, bench, rows — rest 2-3 minutes. These are demanding. Take your time.
Isolation Exercises Curls, tricep work, lateral raises — rest 60-90 seconds. Shorter rest, quicker pace.

If you're breathing hard and not recovered, rest longer. If you're scrolling your phone for 5 minutes between bicep curls, speed it up.

Logging Your Workout

Log every set in the Everfit app as you go. Not after the workout. Not later tonight. As you go.

Finished a set? Enter the weight and reps immediately. This takes 5 seconds and means you'll never forget what you did. Your training history is how I track your progress and adjust your program.

The Flow of a Session

→ Arrive at the gym
→ Warm up (see warmup guide)
→ Open Everfit, start today's workout
→ Do first exercise — log your sets
→ Move to next exercise — log your sets
→ Repeat until program complete
→ Leave

That's it. No guessing. No improvising. The program tells you what to do—you execute and log.

Unsure About an Exercise?

Watch the demo. Every exercise in the app has a video attached. If you're not 100% sure on the movement, watch it before your first set. Better to take 30 seconds to check than to do it wrong for 3 sets.

Open the app. Follow the program. Log as you go. That's the whole system.
