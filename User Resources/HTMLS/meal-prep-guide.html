Meal Prep

The simplest way to stick to your plan without thinking about it.

What It Actually Is

Meal prep is cooking some or all of your meals in advance so you don't have to make decisions when you're hungry, tired, or busy. That's it. You're not becoming a chef. You're removing friction.

The goal isn't perfect meals. It's consistent meals.

The Process

1
Pick one day Sunday works for most people. Block 1-2 hours. Put it in your calendar like any other appointment.
2
Shop from your meal plan Your plan has a shopping list. Use it. Don't improvise. In and out in 30 minutes.
3
Batch cook your proteins Bake all your chicken. Brown all your mince. Cook all your eggs. This is 80% of the work.
4
Prep your carbs Rice, potatoes, oats—whatever your plan calls for. Cook in bulk. Store in containers.
5
Portion into containers Divide everything into meal-sized portions. Label if you want. Fridge for 3-4 days, freezer for longer.

Why It Works Better

Without Prep

  • Daily decisions drain willpower
  • "What should I eat?" 3x/day
  • More likely to order takeaway
  • Ingredients go bad unused
  • Costs more money weekly
  • Eating becomes a task

With Prep

  • Zero daily food decisions
  • Open fridge, grab container, done
  • Plan is automatic, not effortful
  • Nothing goes to waste
  • Cheaper per meal
  • Eating takes 5 minutes

Make It Effortless

Same containers. Get 10-12 identical containers. No hunting for lids. No mismatched sizes. Boring is efficient.

Cook while you wait. Rice in the cooker, chicken in the oven, you're prepping veg. Everything finishes around the same time.

Don't get fancy. Your meals don't need to be Instagram-worthy. Protein + carbs + veg in a container. Repeat. Done.

Start with 3 days. You don't need to prep a full week on day one. Prep Sunday for Mon-Wed. See how it feels. Build from there.

2 hours once beats 30 minutes daily every single time.
