Breakfast Options
Three choices. Pick one. No overthinking.

OPTION 1: EGGS (Minimum 4x per week)

The foundation of your breakfast routine.

Scrambled Fried Poached Omelette Boiled

Pair with: Toast, bacon, mushrooms, tomatoes, or nothing at all.

OPTION 2: OATS (Maximum 2x per week alone)

Good but needs protein to count.

Must pair with: Protein shake, Greek yogurt, or eggs on the side. Oats alone = not enough protein.

Add fruit: Banana, berries, or apple for energy.

OPTION 3: FULL IRISH

Special occasion or weekend treat. Keep protein high, watch the carbs.

Focus on: Eggs, bacon, sausages. Go easy on the toast and beans.

Why Eggs Are King

  • High protein (6-7g per egg)
  • Keeps you full until lunch
  • Takes 5 minutes to cook
  • Cheap and always available
  • Versatile - never gets boring

Why Oats Work (When Done Right)

  • Slow-release energy - no mid-morning crash
  • Fiber keeps digestion moving
  • Easy to prep the night before
  • But MUST add protein source or you'll be hungry by 10am

When Housemates Cook
This happens 3-5 nights a week. Here's the exact system.

The Rule: Eat what they make. You're still on-plan.

The 3 Adjustments:

  1. Portion: Take ONE serving. No seconds unless you're genuinely hungry (not just because it's there).
  2. Extras: Skip the garlic bread, the second helping of cheese, the side of chips if you don't need them.
  3. Drink: Water or diet drink. Not a beer or Coke (unless it's a special occasion).

Common housemate meals:

🍝 Pasta bolognese: Have one bowl, skip the garlic bread

🍛 Curry & rice: Normal portion, skip the naan

🍕 Pizza night: 2-3 slices max, side salad if available

🍔 Burgers: Eat the burger, skip half the chips

🍜 Stir-fry: Load up - this is a good one

You can still tick the dinner checkbox on housemate nights. You ate a real meal with protein. That counts.

Edge Cases (When Shit Gets Weird)
Answers to "but what if..." questions.

Q: Housemate makes pure junk (full fry at 9pm). Do I eat it?

A: Eat it. One serving. Skip the seconds. Tick the box. It's one meal in 21. If it happens regularly, have a chat with them or keep chicken/eggs in the fridge as backup.

Q: Ran out of snacks mid-week. What do I do?

A: Banana + handful of any nuts you can find = fine. Or grab a protein bar from Spar on your lunch break. Or apple from the fruit bowl. Any protein-ish snack beats vending machine crisps.

Q: 1 checkbox Saturday + 2 checkboxes Sunday - still on track?

A: Yes. That's 3 boxes across 2 days. Not ideal, but NOT a fail. You're still in the game. Monday is a fresh start and you haven't "ruined the week."

Q: What if NOTHING on this page applies to my situation?

A: Default rule: Pick something with protein in it, skip the extras, drink water. You're on-plan. Move on.

Cooking? Keep it Stupid Simple
If it takes more than 30 mins, you won't do it.

The formula:

  1. Heat pan with oil
  2. Cook protein (chicken/mince/fish) - about 15 mins
  3. Boil/microwave your carb (rice/pasta/potato)
  4. Steam/microwave veg or have it raw (salad)
  5. Put it on a plate. Season if you want. Eat.

That's it. You're not Gordon Ramsay. You don't need to be.

Real Example: Beef Mince & Pasta
How to build a meal you can actually track.

The 4-Step Method:

  1. Cook meat separately — 150g beef mince in a pan
  2. Cook pasta separately — 120g dry pasta, boiled
  3. Add veg — 60g peppers or spinach
  4. Now you know EXACTLY what you're eating

Why separate? When you mix everything in one pot, you're guessing. When you weigh and cook separately, you have complete control. That's the difference between progress and plateaus.

"Preparation is key to winning at your diet."

The 6 Golden Principles
Eating out? Follow these. No thinking required.
1
The "Protein Anchor"
Never order a meal where carbs are the main event. Scan for "Chicken", "Beef", "Fish". If portion is small, pay for extra meat.
2
The "Adjective Game"
Avoid: Crispy, Battered, Breaded, Creamy, Smothered
Choose: Grilled, Steamed, Roasted, Blackened, Baked
3
Sauce Sovereignty
Sauce "On the Side" always. Dip your fork, don't pour. Chefs drown food in butter/sugar/oil.

⚠ SAUCE MATH: You can lose 200 calories just from sauce in one meal. That's a 20-minute jog. For sauce. Not worth it.

4
One Carb Source Law
Pick your poison, but pick ONE. Burger Bun OR Chips. Rice OR Naan. Never both.
5
The "Beige Alert"
If the plate is one colour (beige), you're in trouble. Force green onto the plate (side salad/veg) and eat it first.
6
Liquid Sniper Defense
Don't drink your calories. Water, Sparkling Water, or Diet Soda only. Save the beer for special occasions.

Preparation Tips
The system that makes this sustainable.
  • Prepare meals the day before — morning you will thank night you
  • Prepare 3-4 meals minimum when cooking — same effort, triple output
  • Cook ingredients separately — easy to weigh, easy to track
  • Get containers with compartments — portion control becomes automatic

Think Weekly, Not Daily
This changes everything.

You have a weekly calorie budget, not a daily one. Think of it like money - overspend Monday, spend less Tuesday. It balances out.

One bad day doesn't ruin the week. It just means you have a bit less budget for the remaining days. Keep going.

Analogy: If you overspent on Monday, would you empty your bank account on Tuesday? No. You'd just be more careful the rest of the week.

Water: The Actual Strategy
How to actually drink 2L when you forget.

The system:

  1. Buy a 500ml water bottle (or reuse any bottle)
  2. Fill it when you wake up - drink some while making breakfast
  3. Take it to work. Refill it at lunch = 2 bottles done
  4. Refill when you get home. Sip during dinner = 3 bottles
  5. Refill before bed. Finish while watching TV = 4 bottles = 2L

Hack: Every time you walk past the water cooler or kitchen, take a drink. Make it automatic.

Still drinking Coke? Switch to Coke Zero/Diet. Same taste, zero calories. Baby steps.

About the Scale
Read this before you weigh yourself.

Weight fluctuates 1-3kg daily. Water, food in stomach, salt intake - all affect the number.

Weigh yourself 4-5 times a week, same time each day (morning, after bathroom, before food).

Trend matters, not individual readings. Are you lower this month than last month? That's progress.

If the number goes up: Don't panic. Check next week. If it's still up, you're eating too much. If it's down, it was just water.
